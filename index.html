About FASE Challenge

Speech-driven 3D facial animation synthesis field is growing fast with large numbers of papers published every year thanks to the power of advanced deep learning algorithms. However, the variety of the methodologies, datasets and evaluation criteria makes it very difficult to compare the state-of-the-art works with each other. The FASE Challenge aims to provide a platform to overcome this challenge by allowing researchers working in this area to test and evaluate their methods using standard set of datasets and subjective and objective evaluation criteria. We are aiming that the results from the challenge will provide a critical look to the current methodologies, datasets and evaluation metrics, provide a fresh understanding and will help to push the field forward. We encourage participation from both academy and industry including computer vision/machine learning and computer graphics/animation communities.

The FASE Challenge includes topics related to speech-driven 3D facial animation for both 3D vertex-based and rigged virtual characters, identity and style/emotion controlled facial animation, deterministic and non-deterministic facial synthesis

approaches, singing facial animation, facial animation across different languages and synthesis of facial motion in group interactions. The specific goals of the challenge are enumerated below:

  • Provide a platform to standardize the comparison and evaluation of state-of-the-art 3D facial animation synthesis approaches
  • Bring together the researchers and practitioners working in this field to discuss the current state-of-the-art and future directions
  • Provide a deeper understanding of the datasets and evaluation metrics available with a critical and fresh look into the current practices
  • Contribute to open science by encouraging sharing code, datasets and evaluation pipelines
  • Encourage new collaborations and synergies among researchers from academy, industry and computer vision/machine learning and computer graphics/animation communities
